## Critical (will lose money or corrupt state)

### 1. Inverted Implication Constraints
- **What**: Rule-based classifiers for price thresholds and rankings do not guarantee that `market_a` is the antecedent and `market_b` is the consequent, leading to inverted constraint matrices.
- **Where**: `services/detector/classifier.py` (lines 148-220, 237-270) and `services/detector/constraints.py` (line 62).
- **Why**: `_check_price_threshold_markets` and `_check_ranking_markets` identify an implication but return a generic type. `build_constraint_matrix` hardcodes `matrix[0][1] = 0` (A=Yes implies B=Yes). If the classifier receives Market 1 (BTC > 100k) and Market 2 (BTC > 90k) in the order `(M2, M1)`, it enforces `90k => 100k`. The system will detect "arbitrage" whenever BTC is between 90k and 100k, buy the higher threshold, sell the lower, and lose 100% of the capital on settlement.
- **Fix**: Update rule-based classifiers to return `antecedent_id` and `consequent_id`. In `constraints.py`, swap the matrix indices if the `market_a_id` does not match the `antecedent_id`.

### 2. Broken Short Accounting and PnL Realization
- **What**: `Portfolio.execute_trade` fails to handle "Buy to Cover" (closing a short) and corrupts `cost_basis`, while `Pipeline` double-counts realized PnL.
- **Where**: `services/simulator/portfolio.py` (lines 48-51) and `services/simulator/pipeline.py` (lines 152-170).
- **Why**: `execute_trade` BUY side logic always adds to `cost_basis`. If a position is -10 (short) and you BUY 10, `cost_basis` becomes `(credit_received) + (cost_to_buy)`. Settlement then calculates `pnl = cost_basis - obligation`, making every covered short look like a massive winner. Additionally, `Pipeline` manually adds to `realized_pnl` while `Portfolio.close_position` also does so, leading to double-counting.
- **Fix**: Implement "Buy to Cover" logic in `Portfolio.execute_trade` that reduces `cost_basis` (credit) proportionally and returns the realized PnL. Remove manual `realized_pnl` updates from `Pipeline.py`.
