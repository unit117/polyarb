## Critical (will lose money or corrupt state)

### 1. Incorrect Unrealized PnL and Positions in Profit for Short Positions
- **What**: `unrealized_pnl` and `positions_in_profit` calculations are incorrect for short positions.
- **Where**: `services/simulator/portfolio.py`, lines 105-108 and 115-118
- **Why**:
    - For short positions (`shares < 0`), `cost_basis` tracks the *credit received*.
    - `unrealized_pnl`: The formula `pnl += shares * current - cost` becomes `(-abs_shares * current_price) - credit_received`, which is `-(abs_shares * current_price + credit_received)`. The correct formula for a short is `credit_received - current_obligation`, i.e., `cost - (abs(shares) * current)`.
    - `positions_in_profit`: The condition `shares * current > cost` will always be false for short positions because `shares * current` will be negative (or zero), while `cost` (credit received) is positive. A short position is in profit if `cost > abs(shares) * current`.
    - This leads to misreporting of portfolio performance and incorrect metrics for open short positions.
